Understanding DNA, Proteins, and Genes

What is DNA?

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid) is the blueprint of life. It carries genetic instructions used in growth, development, and reproduction. Each DNA molecule is made up of nucleotides, which include Adenine (A), Thymine (T), Cytosine (C), and Guanine (G).

What are Genes?

Genes are segments of DNA that code for specific proteins. They determine physical traits, influence susceptibility to diseases, and play a crucial role in inheritance.

How Do Proteins Work?

Proteins are molecules made of amino acids and serve as building blocks for cells, enzymes, and hormones. They are synthesized based on genetic codes present in DNA through transcription and translation processes.

The Role of DNA Analysis in Genetic Disorders

By analyzing DNA sequences, scientists can detect mutations that may cause genetic disorders such as cystic fibrosis, sickle cell anemia, and Huntington’s disease. This project aims to help identify such disorders through advanced sequence analysis.

Research Papers and Notable Scientists

Scientific research has significantly contributed to understanding genetic diseases. Some landmark papers include Watson and Crick’s 1953 paper on the structure of DNA, and modern research on CRISPR technology by Jennifer Doudna and Emmanuelle Charpentier. These studies have paved the way for advancements in genetic engineering and disease treatment.
